How to port your number out of AT&T Wireless

Moving your number from AT&T to a new carrier — or to a number you bought here — takes one PIN, one account number, and a few minutes of prep. Here’s exactly what AT&T requires.

Port-out steps

  1. Keep your AT&T line active — do not cancel service; the new carrier initiates the transfer.
  2. Request your Number Transfer PIN in the myAT&T app or by dialing *PORT (*7678).
  3. Give your new carrier the AT&T account number, the Transfer PIN, and the billing ZIP, name, and address exactly as they appear on your AT&T account.
  4. The carriers validate and schedule the port — wireless transfers typically complete same day to one business day.
  5. Confirm your number is live on the new carrier; your AT&T line closes automatically once the port completes.
Don't cancel first. Keep your AT&T line active until the port completes — the new carrier initiates the transfer, and cancelling early releases the number instead of moving it.

Common rejection risks

What bounces AT&T ports

A wrong or expired Transfer PIN · an inactive or suspended line · a name, address, or ZIP that doesn’t match AT&T’s records. Device financing doesn’t block the port — but the balance still comes due.

Why was my AT&T port rejected?

The most common causes: Wrong NTP, inactive/suspended line, mismatch on account name/address/ZIP, device finance issues not port blockers but can create billing risk.
