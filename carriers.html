Porting FAQ

Asked before every port

How long does it take to port a number?

Local numbers typically port in 1–3 business days. Toll-free numbers usually complete in 1–2 business days. Wireless-to-wireless transfers are often same-day.

Can I use a toll-free number on my cell phone?

Not by porting it — toll-free numbers live on the national toll-free system and can’t become a wireless line, so carriers like Verizon or T-Mobile can’t take them directly. The standard setup is call forwarding: your toll-free number rings straight to any cell or office phone, and callers never know the difference. Local numbers, by contrast, port to wireless carriers normally.

Can I port my number anywhere in the country?

To a wireless or VoIP carrier, yes — geography doesn’t matter, so a Miami 305 number works fine on a phone in Seattle. The one exception is traditional physical landline service, which generally requires the number to match the local rate center. If you’re porting to any modern carrier, you can ignore this.

Will my new carrier treat this as a landline port?

Technically yes — marketplace numbers are classified as wireline, so a wireless carrier processes the move as a landline-to-wireless port rather than a same-day simple port. Much of our inventory is hosted with Bandwidth, the licensed carrier network behind many major US communications brands, and Bandwidth-hosted numbers clear to wireless carriers quickly. Your dashboard shows the exact account number and transfer PIN to give your new carrier, so the request goes through clean the first time.

What if I’m not ready to port yet?

Your number just stays parked and active on our network — parking is free for your first 6 months, then $2.99/month until you move it. Port whenever you’re ready; there’s no deadline and no port-out fee.

Will my number stay active during the transfer?

Yes. Your number keeps working on our network until the moment the new carrier activates it — there is no dead period.

Can I port my number to a VoIP service like RingCentral or Google Voice?

Yes. Every number we sell can be ported to any US or Canadian VoIP provider, or connected directly through call forwarding while you decide.

Does it cost anything to port my number out?

We never charge a port-out fee. Your receiving carrier may have its own activation fees — check with them before starting the transfer.

What if my carrier isn't listed here?

This list covers common carriers, but any US or Canadian wireless, VoIP, or landline carrier can receive your number. If you can name it, we can port to it.

Can I port a number I just bought?

Yes. Numbers are portable as soon as they're activated on our network — typically within minutes of purchase. There is no required holding period.

What can make a port fail?

The usual causes are a mismatched account name, a wrong account number or transfer PIN, or an inactive line. Double-check those details from your dashboard before submitting the request to your new carrier.

Do I lose my number if the port fails?

No. A failed port simply leaves the number where it is — active on our network. Fix the detail that caused the rejection and resubmit; nothing is lost.
